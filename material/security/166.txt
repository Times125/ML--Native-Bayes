KIEV, Oct. 15 (Xinhua) -- The Ukrainian Interior Ministry announced Thursday the establishment of a special police unit to shore up the country's cyber security and combat cyber crimes. "Cyber-crime is a global problem and Ukraine must respond to this challenge. That's why we are creating a special cyber police," Prime Minister Arseniy Yatsenyuk said. The main task of the new unit is to protect citizens, and government agencies against cyber criminals, who can steal their personal data, Yatsenyuk said. Besides, the new cyber bureau, consisting of 400 experts in high-tech sector, will develop measures to prevent cyber terrorism and intellectual property theft, he added. In the first half of 2015, the Interior Ministry has registered 2,383 cyber-crime cases in Ukraine, of which 956 cases were classified as fraud.
