The waters along Tasmania s north-west coastline have taken on a bizarre, glowing appearance in recent days. Photographs taken off Preservation Bay and Rocky Cape showcase bioluminescent waters caused by  (AKA sea sparkle), tiny plankton emitting blue light in self-defence. The phenomenon, which is best seen in calm, warm seas, is foreboding.  The displays are a sign of climate change,  Anthony Richardson, from the CSIRO,  after an occurrence in Tasmania in 2015.
