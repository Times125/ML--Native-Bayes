BRUSSELS, Dec. 9 (Xinhua) -- NATO has trained Iraqi officers on cyber defense to strengthen the country's capabilities in the sector, NATO said in a press release Friday. A total of 16 civil servants from the new Iraqi Computer Incident Response Team were trained in Ankara, Turkey during the course from Nov. 21 to Dec. 2. The program focused on Iraq's cyber security and defense requirements, including theoretical sessions and practical laboratory exercises. This training was delivered as part of the defense capacity building initiative endorsed by NATO allies at the 2014 NATO Summit in Wales.
